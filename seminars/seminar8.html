Seminar VIII

Understanding the Agentic AI

Speaker: Mr. Ralph Vincent Regalado

Date: June 13, 2025

Location: UIC Computer Lab

Reflection

The seminar titled “Understanding Agentic AI: A Progression of Language Model (LLM) Usage” was a deeply engaging and timely session that shed light on the evolving capabilities and design patterns surrounding large language models (LLMs). With AI rapidly transforming various industries, this session helped provide a foundational and forward-looking understanding of how these models are currently used—and how they are becoming increasingly agentic.

The presentation began with a comprehensive overview of LMs, providing context on what language models are, how they are trained, and how they generate language. This gave participants a solid grounding before moving into more applied areas. The speaker highlighted current LM usage trends, including their integration into customer support, education, productivity tools, and creative workflows.

One of the critical discussions involved the limitations of LM usage. Issues such as hallucinations, lack of grounding in real-time data, and overconfidence in incorrect outputs were addressed. These limitations set the stage for introducing concepts like Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), which aims to enhance model accuracy and trustworthiness by integrating external, verifiable sources into the generation process.

We then explored the concept of tool usage in AI, focusing on how LLMs can be connected with APIs, plugins, or web-based tools to accomplish real-world tasks. This naturally led to the exploration of Agentic LMs—language models that can reason, plan, and act semi-autonomously using tools and memory.

The session concluded with a look at Agentic LM design patterns, including looped reasoning, goal decomposition, and chaining multiple models or steps to complete more complex tasks. This opened my eyes to the future of AI systems that are not just assistants but active agents capable of executing complex workflows on behalf of users.

Overall, the seminar was not just informative but transformative in how it framed the future of AI. I left with a deeper understanding of how AI agents are evolving and how I, as a future practitioner or developer, might contribute meaningfully to this rapidly changing landscape.

Key Takeaways

  • Language Models (LLMs) are evolving from passive text generators to active reasoning agents.
  • Understanding the current usage and limitations of LMs is essential to leveraging them effectively.
  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) enhances response accuracy by incorporating external data sources.
  • Tool usage expands the functionality of LMs, allowing them to perform real-world actions beyond text.
  • Agentic LMs represent the next stage in AI: models that can plan, reason, and act autonomously.
  • Design patterns such as chaining, looping, and goal decomposition are central to building intelligent AI agents.
  • There is vast potential for careers in designing, training, and deploying Agentic AI systems.
