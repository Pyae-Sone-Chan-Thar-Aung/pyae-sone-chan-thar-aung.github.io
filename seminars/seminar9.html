Seminar IX

Exploring Web3: Blockchain, DeFi, and the Future of the Internet

Speaker: Ferdie James Nervida, Rod Albores, Ejay Bastes, Ken Berdz Berry

Date: June 14, 2025

Location: UIC Computer Lab

Reflection

The seminar on “Exploring Web3: Blockchain, DeFi, and the Future of the Internet” was a thought-provoking and enlightening experience that broadened my understanding of the next generation of the internet. With the rapid development of digital technologies, this seminar came at a crucial time, allowing us to grasp the direction in which online systems, platforms, and communities are heading.

The session began by outlining the evolution from Web2 to Web3. This transition marks a significant shift from centralized platforms dominated by large corporations (Web2) to decentralized ecosystems that return power and ownership to users (Web3). The concept of decentralization—an integral theme throughout the seminar—was explained clearly and with engaging examples, helping me understand how Web3 aims to solve many of the limitations and ethical concerns posed by current internet structures.

The introduction to blockchain technology was another cornerstone of the discussion. Blockchain was not just presented as a buzzword but as a powerful ledger system that ensures transparency, security, and trust. I appreciated the way the speakers broke down its technical complexity into digestible ideas, emphasizing why it matters—not just for cryptocurrency, but for industries ranging from supply chain to identity verification.

Among the most fascinating parts of the seminar was the deep dive into DeFi (Decentralized Finance). Topics such as lending platforms, decentralized exchanges (DEXs), and dApps (decentralized applications) showcased how blockchain is transforming the financial system. Unlike traditional finance, DeFi offers openness and accessibility, which could provide financial services to the unbanked population globally. This part of the discussion made me consider the socio-economic implications of Web3 and how it might reshape financial inclusion.

Another highlight was the explanation of smart contracts and NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens). The idea of programmable contracts that execute automatically without intermediaries opened my eyes to new possibilities in digital agreements and automation. Similarly, NFTs were explained beyond the hype—showing their relevance not just in art but also in verifying ownership and provenance in various digital and physical assets.

The seminar also presented compelling real-world use cases across art, technology, and finance. Hearing actual applications of Web3 principles made the topic more relatable and grounded. Whether it was artists monetizing their work directly or developers building decentralized apps that solve real problems, the examples gave us a tangible sense of how Web3 is already being implemented.

Finally, the discussion on career paths and builder opportunities in Web3 was particularly inspiring. The speakers shared their experiences and emphasized that this space is still growing, full of opportunities for developers, designers, writers, analysts, and community managers. It encouraged me to think about how I might contribute to or explore this space professionally.

In conclusion, the seminar was not only educational but also motivating. It demystified complex concepts and revealed the human and ethical dimensions of Web3. I left with a sense of curiosity, hope, and responsibility—to not just observe the evolution of the internet, but to participate in building a more open, decentralized, and user-owned digital future.

Key Takeaways

  • Web3 marks a shift from centralized Web2 platforms to decentralized, user-owned ecosystems.
  • Blockchain technology provides transparency, security, and trust across various industries.
  • DeFi (Decentralized Finance) enables open financial systems through dApps, lending protocols, and DEXs.
  • Smart contracts automate digital agreements, eliminating the need for intermediaries.
  • NFTs extend beyond digital art, offering solutions for ownership verification in real and virtual assets.
  • Real-world applications of Web3 are already emerging in finance, tech, and creative industries.
  • Web3 presents career opportunities not only for developers but also for creatives, analysts, and community builders.
  • Understanding and engaging with Web3 early provides a competitive advantage in future digital landscapes.
